# Bitcoin Bottom Index Dashboard

**Live site:** https://brls123.github.io/BBI-Page/  
**中文说明：** [查看中文版 README](./README.zh-CN.md)

A public dashboard for reading Bitcoin market conditions through valuation, sentiment, macro, onchain, and cross-market signals.

## What it does

Bitcoin Bottom Index (BBI) is designed to help answer one practical question:

**Does the current market environment look more like bottom formation, BTC repair, trend continuation, or risk-off stress?**

Instead of relying on a single metric, BBI combines multiple dimensions into one structured dashboard so the market can be read in context rather than through isolated indicators.

## What you can see on the page

The dashboard brings together several layers of information:

- **BBI composite score** for bottom-friendliness and accumulation conditions
- **Market regime classification** such as Deleveraging, BTC Repair, BTC Bull, and Alt Season
- **Macro / technical / fundamental dimensions** to show where strength or weakness is coming from
- **US equities and alt breadth context** to track broader risk appetite and rotation conditions
- **Historical analogs and scenario comparisons** to place the current setup in a longer market framework

## What BBI means

**BBI = Bitcoin Bottom Index**.

It is a composite framework built to estimate how supportive current conditions are for Bitcoin bottom formation and accumulation.

It is **not** a signal for calling exact tops or bottoms tick by tick. Instead, it is meant to improve decision quality by identifying whether the broader backdrop looks:

- deeply discounted
- stress-heavy but stabilizing
- neutral or mixed
- trend-supportive
- overheated or risk-heavy

## How the index is constructed

BBI combines multiple categories of signals rather than depending on one chart or one sentiment reading.

### 1. Valuation
Valuation indicators help judge whether Bitcoin looks historically cheap or expensive.

Examples include:

- **AHR999**
- **Mayer Multiple**

### 2. Sentiment
Sentiment indicators help show whether the market is in fear, panic, neutrality, or greed.

Example:

- **Fear & Greed Index**

### 3. Macro conditions
Bitcoin does not trade in isolation. Liquidity, rates, dollar strength, volatility, and broader risk appetite all affect whether a bottom is likely to hold.

### 4. Cycle / technical structure
This layer helps distinguish between breakdown, stabilization, repair, and stronger trend alignment.

### 5. Onchain context
Onchain signals help identify whether long-term holder behavior and network conditions are supportive, mixed, or weak.

### 6. Cross-market context
The dashboard also tracks external confirmation through:

- **US equities / risk appetite**
- **Altcoin breadth**
- **BTC dominance and rotation conditions**

This matters because some environments support BTC-led recovery, while others favor broad participation or continued deleveraging.

## Regimes on the dashboard

To make the market easier to interpret, the page maps current conditions into a few intuitive regimes:

- **Deleveraging** — stress, weak breadth, fragile risk appetite
- **BTC Repair** — Bitcoin stabilizes first while broader participation is still limited
- **BTC Bull** — stronger trend alignment and healthier market structure
- **Alt Season** — broader rotation beyond BTC with expanding participation

## Why this page is useful

This dashboard is built for people who want a more structured way to read Bitcoin conditions, especially around questions like:

- Is this environment bottom-friendly?
- Is BTC repairing or trending?
- Are alts actually participating yet?
- Are macro and equities helping or hurting?
- Does the current setup resemble past recovery phases or stress windows?

## Important note

BBI is an analytical framework, not financial advice. It is designed to improve market context and probabilistic thinking, not to guarantee outcomes.
